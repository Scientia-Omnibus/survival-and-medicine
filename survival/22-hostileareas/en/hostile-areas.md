> You might have to cross hostile areas to get into safety and survive.

Movement through hostile areas is the most dangerous phase. Move at night for concealment, except in hazardous terrain or dense vegetation.

## Areas to Avoid

| Feature | Reason |
|---|---|
| Obstacles and barriers | Slow movement, increase exposure |
| Roads and trails | High traffic, easy to track |
| Inhabited areas | Risk of detection |
| Waterways and bridges | Natural lines of drift, monitored |
| Natural lines of drift | Predictable paths |
| Man-made structures | Frequently observed |
| All civilian and military personnel | Direct threat |

## Hide Site Selection — BLISS

| Letter | Meaning |
|---|---|
| B | Blends in with surroundings |
| L | Low in silhouette |
| I | Irregular in shape |
| S | Small in size |
| S | Secluded |

Do not use existing buildings or shelters. Crawl into thick vegetation. Build shelter only in cold weather or desert environments.

## Hide Site Activities

- Approach using a buttonhook or deceptive technique. Conduct a listening halt before entering.
- Limit activities to security, resting, camouflaging, and planning.
- Use a buddy system for camouflage checks.
- Do not occupy longer than 24 hours. Hide by day, move by night.
- Restrict movement to under 45 cm (18 in) above ground.
- No fires or food preparation. Sterilize site before leaving.

## Five-Point Contingency Plan

| Question |
|---|
| Who is going? |
| Where are they going? |
| How long will they be gone? |
| What to do if hit or do not return on time? |
| Where to go if anyone is hit? |

## Movement Planning

- Choose the next hide area first, then primary and alternate routes.
- Use one or two radical direction changes. Avoid straight lines.
- Pick routes with best cover, fewest obstacles, lowest contact likelihood.
- Use azimuths, distances, checkpoints, and corridors.
- Plan rally points and rendezvous points along the route.

## Hole-Up Areas

After 3-4 days of movement, move to a hole-up area near water. Do not occupy longer than 72 hours.

| Activity | Notes |
|---|---|
| Rest and recuperate | Maintain security at all times |
| Gather food | Nuts, berries, vegetables; keep traps camouflaged |
| Get water | Avoid leaving tracks on banks; walk on hard rocks or logs |
| Set fishing devices | Stakeouts below water surface |
| Locate fire site | Well away from hide site; camouflage and sterilize after use |

## Border Crossings

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Occupy hide site near border; reconnoiter crossing site |
| 2 | Surveil site for at least 24 hours |
| 3 | Sketch site — note terrain, obstacles, guard rotations, sensors |
| 4 | Cross at night; set up hide site on far side |
| 5 | Locate friendly positions without revealing presence |
| 6 | Surveil linkup site until certain personnel are friendly |
| 7 | Make contact in daylight — unarmed, no equipment, ID ready |
| 8 | One person makes contact; observer provides security |
| 9 | Stand from behind cover, hands overhead, state identity |
| 10 | Follow instructions; avoid tactical answers or revealing others |

> If moving to a neutral country, you are surrendering to that power and become a detained person.

## Linkup at Forward Edge of Battle Area

If caught between forces, hide and let friendly lines pass over you. Linkup is same as border crossing but more cautious — frontline personnel may shoot first.

## Linkup with Friendly Patrols

- Observe perimeter and wait for a patrol to move toward you.
- Signal from cover with something white or any article of clothing.
- Call out an unmistakably American greeting at 25-50 meters (83-165 ft).
- Ensure enough light for identification.

> Whatever linkup method used, exercise extreme caution. From the perspective of friendly forces, you are hostile until positively identified.
