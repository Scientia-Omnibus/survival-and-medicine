## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Bight | Simple bend of rope where the rope does not cross itself |
| Dressing the knot | Aligning all knot parts properly. Neglecting this can reduce strength by 50%. |
| Fraps | Tightening lashings by looping rope perpendicularly around the wraps that hold spars together |
| Lashings | Tying two or three spars together using wraps and fraps; begin and end with clove hitches |
| Lay | The twist direction of the rope |
| Loop | Formed by crossing the running end over or under the standing end to create a ring |
| Pig tail | Remaining running end after tying the knot; keep under 10 cm (4 in) |
| Running end | The free or working end of the rope used to tie the knot |
| Standing end | The static part of the rope |
| Turn | A loop around an object with the running end continuing in the opposite direction to the standing end |
| Whipping | Wrapping the rope end tightly with small cord or tape to prevent untwisting |
| Wraps | Simple wraps of rope around poles; begin and end with clove hitches |

## Basic Knots

| Knot | Use | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Half-hitch | Simple securing | Tends to undo without load. Replace with overhand as finishing knot. |
| Overhand | General stopper knot; temporary whipping | Reduces rope strength by 55%. Replaces half-hitch as finishing knot. |
| Square | Tying two ropes of equal diameter | Two reversed overhand knots (right over left, left over right). Secure with overhand on both ends. Easy to inspect. |
| Round turn and two half-hitches | Anchor knot for high loads; rope to pole or tree | Does not jam under load. Main anchor knot for one-rope bridges. |
| Clove hitch | Fastening rope to a tree or pipe | Easy anchor knot. Tension must remain or it slips. Add extra loop to prevent slipping. |
| Sheep shank | Shortening a rope; bypassing a weak spot | Temporary unless eyes are fastened to standing part on both ends. |
| Double sheet bend | Joining two ropes of equal or unequal diameter; wet rope | Will not slip or draw tight under load. Can join multiple ropes to one. |
| Prusik | Tying a short rope around a longer rope for climbing | Slides when slack, grips under tension. Can be tied with end or bight of rope. |
| Bowline | Creating a fixed loop that will not slip or tighten under strain | Was standard rescue knot. Loop placed around body. |
| Figure 8 | Main rescue and anchor knot | Stronger than bowline. Easier to tie and check. Harder to untie when wet. Prevents end from slipping through fastenings. |

## Lashings

| Type | Use |
|---|---|
| Shears lashing | Tying two spars at an angle to form shears |
| Square lashing | Tying two spars at right angles |
| Tripod lashing | Tying three spars together for tripod construction |

> Tighten all knots before putting them under load. A loosely tied knot can deform under strain and slip or untie.
