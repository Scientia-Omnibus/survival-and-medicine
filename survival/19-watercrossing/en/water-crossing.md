> In a survival situation, you may have to cross a water obstacle. It may be in the form of a river, a stream, a lake, a bog, quicksand, quagmire, or muskeg. Even in the desert, flash floods occur, making streams an obstacle. Whatever the obstacle, you need to know how to cross it safely.

## RIVERS AND STREAMS

Before attempting a crossing, find a high place or climb a tree to survey the waterway.

### Good Crossing Locations

| Location | Reason |
|---|---|
| Level stretch where river breaks into channels | Two or three narrow channels easier than one wide river. |
| Shallow bank or sandbar | Select point upstream so current carries you to it if you lose footing. |
| Course downstream at 45° angle | Cross current diagonally. |

### Hazardous Areas to Avoid

| Hazard | Danger |
|---|---|
| Obstacles on opposite side | May hinder travel after crossing. |
| Ledge of rocks across river | Indicates dangerous rapids or canyons. |
| Deep or rapid waterfall / deep channel | Never ford directly above or near such hazards. |
| Rocky places | Submerged rocks are slick. Falling may cause serious injury. |
| Estuary | Wide, strong currents, subject to tides. Go upstream for easier crossing. |
| Eddies | Powerful backward pull can drag you under. |

### Fording a Swift Stream

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Remove pants and shirt to reduce water's pull. Keep footgear on for protection and footing. |
| 2 | Tie clothing and articles to top of rucksack so all items stay together if released. |
| 3 | Carry pack high on shoulders. Ensure it can be removed quickly. |
| 4 | Find a pole 5–10cm diameter, 2–2.5m long. Plant upstream to break current. |
| 5 | Cross at 45° angle downstream. Plant feet firmly. Move pole forward but keep it upstream. |

### Group Crossing

| Number of People | Method |
|---|---|
| 2+ | Heaviest person on downstream end of pole, lightest on upstream. Upstream person breaks current. |
| 3+ with rope | Tie all together. Rope length must be 3× stream width. |

## RAFT TYPES

### Poncho Rafts

| Type | Capacity | Construction |
|---|---|---|
| Brush raft | ~115 kg (253 lbs) | Two ponchos, fresh green brush, two small saplings, rope/vine. Brush core between ponchos. |
| Australian poncho raft | ~35 kg (77 lbs) | Two ponchos, two rucksacks, two 1.5m poles. Equipment between poles, rolled tightly inside ponchos. |
| Poncho donut raft | Variable | Framework circle of saplings/vines wrapped in one poncho. Rope to tow. |

### Log Raft

Use dry, dead, standing trees. Spruce (polar/subpolar regions) makes best rafts. Use pressure bars lashed at each end.

### Launching

| Precautions | Detail |
|---|---|
| Avoid punctures | Do not drag raft on ground. |
| Test flotation | Let raft rest on water a few minutes before crossing. |
| Deep water | Push raft in front while swimming. These rafts do not support full body weight — use as float. |
| Very cold water | Do not ford. Felling a tree to bridge or build a larger raft. |

## FLOTATION DEVICES

| Device | Method |
|---|---|
| Trousers | Knot legs, close fly, swing to trap air, press waistband underwater. Wet trousers first to trap air better. |
| Empty containers | Lash gas cans, water jugs, ammo cans together. Use only in slow-moving water. |
| Plastic bags and ponchos | Fill bags with air. Roll green vegetation tightly inside poncho (~20cm diameter). Wear around waist or across shoulder. |
| Logs | Test first — palm logs sink even when dead. Two logs tied 60cm apart: sit between them. |
| Cattails | Bundle stalks 25cm+ diameter. Air cells keep it afloat until rots. |

## OTHER WATER OBSTACLES

### Bogs, Quagmire, Muskeg, Quicksand

| Obstacle | Technique |
|---|---|
| Bog | Lie face down, arms and legs spread. Use flotation device or create air pockets in clothing. Swim or pull across slowly, keeping body horizontal. |
| Swamp | Vegetated areas are usually firm enough. Open mud/water: swim or crawl. |
| Quicksand | Test with small stone (it sinks). Lie face down, spread arms and legs, move slowly. |

### Mangrove Swamps

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Inland side | Wait for low tide. Look for narrow grove, work seaward. |
| Seaward side | Work inland along streams or channels. |
| Crocodiles | If near, leave water and scramble over mangrove roots. |
| Food | Gather from tidal pools or tree roots. |

### Vegetation Obstacles

Stay near surface. Use breaststroke with shallow leg and arm motion. Remove plants as you would clothing. Rest by floating on back.
