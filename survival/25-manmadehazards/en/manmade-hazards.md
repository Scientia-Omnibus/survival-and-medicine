> Nuclear, chemical, and biological hazards intensify survival problems. Awareness of hazards, knowledge of protective measures, and application of common sense can keep you alive.

---

## The Nuclear Environment

### Effects of Nuclear Weapons

| Effect | Description |
|---|---|
| Blast | Rapid air movement; hurls debris, collapses structures and lungs |
| Thermal radiation | Heat and light from fireball; causes burns, fires, flash blindness |
| Initial nuclear radiation | Gamma rays and neutrons in first minute; causes cell damage, nausea, death |
| Residual radiation | All radiation after 1 minute; includes induced radiation and fallout |

### Types of Nuclear Bursts

| Type | Characteristics | Hazard |
|---|---|---|
| Subsurface | Underground or underwater; effects remain near crater | Low radioactive hazard |
| Airburst | In air above target; maximum immediate effect | Highest immediate danger |
| Surface | On ground or water; large amounts of fallout | Greatest long-term hazard |

### Nuclear Injuries

| Type | Cause | Protection |
|---|---|---|
| Blast | Overpressure, debris, being thrown | Cover and distance |
| Thermal | Heat and light | Cover, distance, clothing covering skin |
| Radiation | Neutrons, gamma, alpha, beta | Time, distance, shielding |

### Residual Radiation

- **Induced radiation:** Highly radioactive area under the fireball. Avoid traveling into it.
- **Fallout:** Radioactive soil, water, and weapon fragments. Particles emit alpha, beta, and gamma radiation. Most significant radiation hazard after the first minute.

### Countermeasures

| Method | Principle |
|---|---|
| Time | Radiation dose is cumulative. Spend minimal time in contaminated areas. Radioactivity decays by factor of 10 every 7-fold time increase. |
| Distance | Radiation decreases by square of distance. Doubling distance = 1/4 the dose. |
| Shielding | Denser materials provide better absorption. Most important protection method. |

### Shielding Material Half-Value Thickness

| Material | Thickness to reduce radiation by 50% |
|---|---|
| Steel | 0.7 cm (0.3 in) |
| Concrete | 3.5 cm (1.4 in) |
| Brick | 5 cm (2 in) |
| Soil | 5 cm (2 in) |
| Water | 5 cm (2 in) |
| Wood | 15 cm (6 in) |

Each additional half-value layer halves remaining radiation. 1 m (3 ft) of dirt reduces 1,000 cGy/hr outside to ~0.5 cGy/hr inside.

### Shelter

- Best: underground shelter covered by 1 m (3 ft) of earth
- Next best: caves, tunnels, storm cellars, culverts, basements, stone/mud buildings
- Dig trenches from prone position; stack dirt for shielding
- Keep body covered to prevent beta burns
- Decontaminate materials brought inside
- Wash thoroughly; if no water, wipe skin with clean cloth

### Exposure Timetable

| Time Period | Action |
|---|---|
| Days 1-6 | Complete isolation |
| Day 3 | Brief exposure for water (max 30 min) |
| Day 7 | One exposure (max 30 min) |
| Day 8 | One exposure (max 1 hour) |
| Days 9-12 | Exposure of 2-4 hours |
| Day 13+ | Normal operation with rest in protected shelter |

> If forced to move earlier, keep exposure as brief as possible. Decontaminate at every stop.

### Water Procurement

| Source | Precautions |
|---|---|
| Springs, wells, underground | Natural filtration; safest |
| Pipes or containers in abandoned buildings | Free from radioactive particles |
| Snow 15 cm (6 in) below surface | Safe |
| Streams and rivers | Relatively safe after several days; filter through sediment holes |
| Standing water (lakes, ponds) | Heavily contaminated; use settling technique with clean dirt |

### Food Procurement

| Food Type | Safety | Preparation |
|---|---|---|
| Sealed combat rations | Safe | Use as-is |
| Processed food in sealed containers | Safe | Wash or remove container before handling |
| Underground vegetables (potatoes, carrots) | Safest local food | Scrub and peel |
| Peelable fruits and vegetables | Second choice | Wash and peel |
| Smooth-skinned produce | Third choice | Washing removes ~90% of contamination |
| Rough-surfaced plants (lettuce) | Last resort | Cannot effectively decontaminate |
| Animal meat | Use if needed | Skin carefully; cut meat away from bone (leave 3 mm); discard organs; cook very well done |
| Fish and aquatic animals | Extreme emergency only | More contaminated than land animals |
| Eggs | Safe | Even if laid during fallout period |
| Milk | Avoid | Animals absorb radioactivity from plants |

## Biological Environments

### Agent Categories

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Pathogens (germs) | Living microorganisms: bacteria, rickettsiae, fungi, viruses. Incubation hours to months. |
| Toxins | Naturally produced poisons. Effects appear immediately. Can penetrate unbroken skin. |

### Routes of Entry

- Respiratory tract
- Breaks in skin
- Digestive tract

### Detection

Biological agents are undetectable by the senses. Look for delivery means:

| Means | Signs |
|---|---|
| Bursting-type munitions | Small cloud of liquid or powder; little blast damage |
| Spray tanks or generators | Aerosol cloud from aircraft or vehicles |
| Vectors | Large infestations of mosquitoes, fleas, lice, ticks |
| Environmental signs | Unusual substances on ground or vegetation; sick plants or animals |

### Protection

- Maintain immunizations
- Keep face covered with cloth
- Wear uniform and gloves to prevent vector bites
- Button clothing fully; tuck trousers into boots
- Practice high standards of hygiene
- Bathe with soap when possible
- Wash clothing in hot, soapy water or lay in bright sunlight

### Shelter

- Do not build in depressions (aerosols concentrate there)
- Avoid areas with dense vegetation
- Place entrance at 90-degree angle to prevailing wind
- Keep shelter clean

### Water and Food

- Sealed containers safest; wash before opening
- Boil non-sealed water for at least 10 minutes
- Local plants and animals only in extreme emergency
- Boil all food for at least 10 minutes; do not fry, bake, or roast

## Chemical Environments

### Detection Indicators

| Sense | Indicator |
|---|---|
| Smell | New-cut grass or hay (choking agents); almonds (blood agents) |
| Sight | Oily patches on leaves; mist or fog after burst; colored substances |
| Sound | Muffled shell or bomb detonations |
| Physical | Tears, difficult breathing, choking, itching, coughing, dizziness |

### Protection Steps

1. Use protective equipment (mask and overgarment)
2. Give self-aid when contaminated
3. Avoid contaminated areas
4. Decontaminate equipment and body as soon as possible

### Shelter

- Move out of contaminated area crosswind or upwind if possible
- Build in clearing away from vegetation
- Remove topsoil in shelter area
- Keep entrance closed, oriented 90 degrees to wind
- Do not burn contaminated wood — smoke will be toxic

### Water and Food

- Sealed containers safest; decontaminate before opening
- Underground water pipes as alternative
- Rainwater or snow if no contamination evident
- Check for: foreign odors (garlic, mustard, almonds), oily spots, dead fish or animals
- Boil or purify all water
- Sealed combat rations safest
- Decontaminate all containers before opening
- Avoid plants from contaminated areas; avoid sick animals
- Use protective gloves and clothing when handling local food sources
