> Water is one of your most urgent needs in a survival situation. You can't live long without it, especially in hot areas where you lose water rapidly through perspiration. Even in cold areas, you need a minimum of 2 liters of water each day to maintain efficiency.
>
> More than three-fourths of your body is composed of fluids. Your body loses fluid because of heat, cold, stress, and exertion. To function effectively, you must replace the fluid your body loses. So, one of your first goals is to obtain an adequate supply of water.

---

### WATER SOURCES

Almost any environment has water present to some degree.

| Environment | Source | How to Obtain | Remarks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frigid areas | Snow and ice | Melt and purify | Do not eat without melting — reduces body temperature. Gray/opaque sea ice is salty. Bluish crystalline ice has little salt. |
| At sea | Seawater | Use desalinator | Do not drink without desalting! |
| At sea | Rain | Catch in tarps or containers | Wash salt off tarps in sea first |
| Beach | Groundwater | Dig hole; heat rocks; drop hot rocks in water; hold cloth over hole to absorb steam; wring out | Alternative: fill container with seawater, boil, hold cloth over to absorb steam, wring |
| Beach | Fresh water | Dig behind first sand dunes | Collects freshwater |
| Desert | Groundwater | Dig in valleys, dry riverbeds, cliff bases, damp sand, green vegetation | Water beneath original valley floor in dune belts |
| Desert | Barrel cactus | Cut off top, mash pulp, suck juice | Do not eat pulp. Needs machete to cut through spines and tough rind. |
| Desert | Rock depressions | Collect from pools or fissures | Periodic rainfall collects in rocks |
| Desert | Rock fissures | Insert tubing and siphon, or lower container | |
| Desert | Porous rock | Insert tubing and siphon | |
| Desert | Condensation on metal | Absorb with cloth, wring out | Night/day temp variation causes condensation |

**Signs of water in the desert:**

- Trails converge toward water. Follow direction of convergence. Look for campfire ashes, animal droppings, trampled terrain.
- Birds circle over water holes. Their flight at dawn/dusk is fast and close to ground. Bird tracks or chirping in evening/morning may indicate nearby water.

**Other water sources:**

| Source | Method |
|---|---|
| Heavy dew | Tie rags/grass tufts around ankles, walk through dew-covered grass before sunrise, wring into container. Can collect up to 1 liter per hour. |
| Bees/ants into tree hole | Siphon with tubing or scoop with improvised dipper. Stuff cloth to absorb, then wring. |
| Tree crotches or rock crevices | Siphon or absorb with cloth. Bird droppings near rock cracks may indicate water. |
| Green bamboo | Bend stalk, tie down, cut off top. Water drips overnight. Old cracked bamboo may also contain water. |
| Banana/plantain/sugarcane | Cut tree leaving 30cm stump, scoop out center bowl-shaped. Roots fill hollow. First 3 fillings bitter. Supplies water up to 4 days. Cover to keep out insects. |
| Tropical vines | Cut notch high as possible, cut vine close to ground, catch dripping liquid. Ensure vine is not poisonous. |
| Young green coconuts | Milk is good thirst quencher. Mature brown coconut milk contains oil that acts as laxative — drink in moderation. |
| Air plants (American tropics) | Strain water from overlapping leaves through cloth to remove debris. |
| Plants with moist pulpy centers | Cut section, squeeze/smash pulp, catch liquid. |
| Plant roots | Dig/pry out, cut into short pieces, smash pulp, catch liquid. |
| Fleshy leaves/stems/stalks (bamboo) | Cut or notch stalks at base of joint to drain liquid. |
| Specific trees | See table below. |

| Tree | How to Obtain Water |
|---|---|
| Palms (buri, coconut, sugar, rattan, nips) | Bruise a lower frond and pull it down; tree will "bleed" at injury |
| Traveler's tree (Madagascar) | Water collects in cuplike sheath at base of leaves |
| Umbrella tree (western tropical Africa) | Water from leaf bases and roots |
| Baobab tree (northern Australia, Africa) | Water collects in bottle-like trunk during wet season; often found after weeks of dry weather |

> **CAUTION: Do not keep plant sap longer than 24 hours. It begins fermenting and becomes dangerous.**

> **CAUTION: Do not drink any liquid that is sticky, milky, or bitter tasting.**

> **CAUTION: Do not substitute the following harmful fluids for water.**

| Fluid | Remarks |
|---|---|
| Alcoholic beverages | Dehydrate the body and cloud judgment |
| Urine | Contains harmful body wastes; ~2% salt |
| Blood | Salty, considered food; requires body fluids to digest; may transmit disease |
| Seawater | ~4% salt; takes ~2 liters body fluids to rid waste from 1 liter seawater; can cause death |

### STILL CONSTRUCTION

Stills draw moisture from the ground and plant material. It takes about 24 hours to get 0.5 to 1 liter of water.

**ABOVEGROUND STILLS**

| Type | Materials | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Vegetation bag still | Clear plastic bag, green leafy vegetation, small rock, sunny slope | Fill bag 1/2-3/4 with vegetation, add rock, seal. Place mouth downhill on slope in full sun. Water condenses and collects around rock. Change vegetation after extracting most water. |
| Transpiration bag still | Plastic bag, tube, leafy tree limb | Tie bag over leafy limb with tube inserted, seal airtight around branch. Tie limb end below bag mouth. Same limb usable 3-5 days. |

> **CAUTION: Do not use poisonous vegetation. It will provide poisonous liquid.**

**BELOWGROUND STILL**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Dig a bowl-shaped hole ~1m across and 60cm deep in a spot with moist soil that gets sunlight most of the day |
| 2 | Dig a sump in center deep enough for your container to stand upright |
| 3 | Anchor tubing to container bottom with a loose overhand knot |
| 4 | Place container upright in sump |
| 5 | Extend free end of tubing up, over, and beyond the hole's lip |
| 6 | Cover hole with clear plastic sheet, weight edges with soil |
| 7 | Place a rock in center of plastic sheet |
| 8 | Lower plastic into hole to ~40cm below ground level, forming an inverted cone with rock at apex directly over container |
| 9 | Add more soil to edges to hold plastic securely |
| 10 | Plug tube when not in use to prevent evaporation and keep insects out |

Drink using tube as straw without disturbing still. For polluted water, dig a trough ~25cm from lip, pour polluted water in it — soil filters it as the still draws. You need at least three stills to meet daily water needs. The vegetation bag produces the best yield.

### WATER PURIFICATION

Rainwater collected in clean containers or plants is usually safe. Purify water from lakes, ponds, swamps, springs, or streams, especially near human settlements or in the tropics.

| Method | Dosage | Wait Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boiling | Rolling boil | Immediate | Safest method; kills all pathogens including Cryptosporidium |
| 2% tincture of iodine | 5 drops clear water / 10 drops cloudy or cold water | 30 min | |
| 10% povidone-iodine | 2 drops | 30 min | Civilian 2%: 10 drops. Cold/clear: 60 min. Very cold/cloudy: add 4 drops, 60 min. |
| Chlorine bleach (5.25% Na hypochlorite) | 2 drops per canteen | 30 min | Cold/cloudy: 60 min. Check available sodium hypochlorite level. |
| Potassium permanganate | 3 small crystals per liter | 30 min | Bright pink = safe. Dark pink = too much (dilute or use as antiseptic). Red = antifungal solution. |

After purifying, partially unscrew cap and turn canteen upside down to rinse unpurified water from the threads.

**Waterborne Pathogens**

| Pathogen | Disease | Symptoms |
|---|---|---|
| Giardia | Giardiasis (beaver fever) | Explosive watery diarrhea, severe cramps, 7-14 days |
| Cryptosporidium | Cryptosporidiosis | More severe and prolonged than giardiasis; no cure but time; only boiling or microfiltration works |
| Dysentery | | Severe prolonged diarrhea, bloody stools, fever, weakness |
| Cholera | | Profuse watery diarrhea, vomiting, leg cramps |
| Typhoid | | Fever, headache, loss of appetite, constipation, bowel bleeding |
| Hepatitis A | | Diarrhea, abdominal pain, jaundice, dark urine |
| Flukes | | Bore into bloodstream, live as parasites |
| Leeches | | Attach to throat/nose, suck blood, create wounds |

### WATER FILTRATION

If water is muddy, stagnant, or foul-smelling:

| Method | Action |
|---|---|
| Settling | Place in container and let stand for 12 hours |
| Filtering | Pour through layers of sand, crushed rock, charcoal, or cloth in bamboo, hollow log, or clothing |

Filtration only clears water and improves taste. You must still purify it. Add charcoal from your fire to remove odor; let stand 45 minutes before drinking.
