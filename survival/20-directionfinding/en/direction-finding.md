> In a survival situation, you will be extremely fortunate if you happen to have a map and compass. If you do have these two pieces of equipment, you will most likely be able to move toward help. If you are not proficient in using a map and compass, you must take the steps to gain this skill. There are several methods by which you can determine direction by using the sun and the stars. These methods, however, will give you only a general direction. You can come up with a more nearly true direction if you know the terrain of the territory or country.

## USING THE SUN AND SHADOWS

The sun always rises in the east and sets in the west, but not exactly due east or due west. Shadows move opposite the sun. In the Northern Hemisphere, shadows move west to east and point north at noon. In the Southern Hemisphere, shadows indicate south at noon.

### Shadow-Tip Method

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Place a 1m (3ft) straight stick in level ground. Mark the shadow tip with a stone. This first mark is always **west** everywhere on earth. |
| 2 | Wait 10–15 minutes. Mark the new shadow tip position. This is **east**. |
| 3 | Draw a straight line through both marks for an approximate east-west line. |
| 4 | Stand with first mark (west) to your left, second mark (east) to your right. You are now facing **north**. |

**Alternate method (more accurate):** Set up stick and mark first shadow in morning. Draw an arc through the mark around the stick. In afternoon, when shadow touches the arc, make second mark. Line through both marks gives accurate east-west line.

### Watch Method

| Hemisphere | Method |
|---|---|
| Northern | Hold watch horizontal. Point hour hand at sun. Bisect angle between hour hand and 12-o'clock mark = north-south line. |
| Southern | Point 12-o'clock mark at sun. Midpoint between 12 and hour hand = north-south line. |

### 24-Hour Clock Method

| Hemisphere | Method |
|---|---|
| Northern | Divide military time by 2. Point this "hour" at sun. 12-o'clock points north. |
| Southern | Point 12 at sun. The resulting "hour" from division points south. |

## USING THE MOON

The moon reflects the sun's light. As it orbits earth on a 28-day cycle, the shape of reflected light varies.

| Condition | Direction |
|---|---|
| Moon rises before sunset | Illuminated side = west. |
| Moon rises after midnight | Illuminated side = east. |

## USING THE STARS

### Northern Sky

| Constellation | How to Use |
|---|---|
| Big Dipper (Ursa Major) | Outer lip stars (pointer stars) point to Polaris (North Star). Draw line from bottom to top pointer star, extend ~5× the distance between them. |
| Cassiopeia (Lazy W) | Five stars forming a "W." Bisect angle on lazy side. Extend line ~5× the distance from bottom to top. North Star is between Cassiopeia and Big Dipper. |
| Polaris (North Star) | Last star of Little Dipper's handle. Remains stationary (rotates 1.08° around celestial pole). Found at same angle above horizon as your latitude. |

### Southern Sky

| Constellation | How to Use |
|---|---|
| Southern Cross (Crux) | Four brightest stars form a cross. Long axis points south. Imagine distance 4.5–5× between these stars down to horizon. |
| Pointer stars | Left of Southern Cross. Help distinguish True Cross from False Cross. The area between them (coal sac) is dark and devoid of stars. |

## IMPROVISED COMPASSES

### Magnetizing Methods

| Method | Steps |
|---|---|
| Silk or hair | Stroke ferrous metal slowly in one direction on silk or through hair. |
| Magnet | Stroke repeatedly at one end with magnet. Always stroke in one direction only. |
| Battery and wire | Minimum 2V battery. Form coil with insulated wire. Touch ends to battery terminals. Insert and remove metal object from coil repeatedly. |

### Suspension Methods

| Method | Detail |
|---|---|
| String/hair | Suspend magnetized needle from nonmetallic string. |
| Floatation | Float on small piece of wood, cork, or leaf in water. |

### Elaborated Compass

Use sewing needle (broken in half), plastic container bottom, and pen silver tip. One half becomes pointer, other half pivot. Attach pointer center to pen tip. Magnetize one end. Rest on pivot point.

## OTHER DIRECTION INDICATORS

| Indicator | Northern Hemisphere | Southern Hemisphere |
|---|---|---|
| Tree moss | More lush on south side (not reliable — moss grows all around) | More lush on north side |
| Tree rings | Wider spacing on south side (toward equator) | Wider spacing on north side |
| Slope vegetation | North-facing: cooler, damper, retains snow patches. South-facing: loses snow first. | Opposite |
| Prevailing wind | Helpful if prevailing direction is known | Helpful if prevailing direction is known |
