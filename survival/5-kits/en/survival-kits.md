# Chapter 5: Kits

> Even the smallest survival kit, if properly prepared, is invaluable when faced with a survival problem. When the situation arises you might not be able to just buy what you need - so it is good to have some essentials for these cases.

### SURVIVAL KITS

The environment is the key to the types of items you will need in your survival kit. How much equipment you put in your kit depends on how you will carry the kit. A kit carried on your body will have to be smaller than one carried in a vehicle. Keep the most important items on your body. For example, your map and compass should always be on your body, as should a lighter and a multi-tool.

In preparing your survival kit, select items that are multipurpose, compact, lightweight, durable, and most importantly, functional. An item is not good if it looks great but does not do what it was designed for. Items should complement each other from layer to layer. A signal mirror in your pocket can be backed up by pen flares and a signal panel in your rucksack.

Your survival kit need not be elaborate. You need only functional items that will meet your needs and a case to hold the items. The container should be:

- Water-repellent or waterproof
- Easy to carry or attach to your body
- Suitable to accept various-sized components
- Durable

Your survival kit should be broken down into categories, each containing items that allow you to sustain your basic needs.

### Fire

| Item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Lighter | Quick ignition |
| Metal match | Reliable spark in wet conditions |
| Waterproof matches | Fire starting in rain |
| Magnesium bar | Produces high-temperature sparks |
| Candle | Sustained flame, light source |
| Magnifying lens | Fire starting with sunlight |

|---|---|

| Non-lubricated condoms | Emergency water transport/storage |

| Povidone-iodine drops | Water purification |
| Cravats | Multi-purpose cloth for water collection |
| Sponges | Collect water from surfaces |
| Small plastic or rubber tubing | Siphon or drinking tube |
| Collapsible canteens or water bags | Water storage |
