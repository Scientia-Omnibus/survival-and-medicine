> When dealing with local people, accept, respect, and adapt to their ways.

## Contact with Local People

Study cultural aspects of your area before deployment. People may be friendly, unfriendly, or indifferent. If known friendly, maintain courtesy and respect. If unknown or hostile, avoid contact and leave no sign.

### Key Behaviors

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Initial contact | Wait until only one person is near; let them approach first |
| General attitude | Friendly, courteous, patient |
| Body language | Smile often; avoid sudden or threatening movements |
| Weapons | Do not display |
| Pace | Approach slowly; do not rush |
| Trading | Use salt, tobacco, silver, paper money discreetly; do not overpay |
| Language | Use sign language; learn a few words of the local language |
| Taboo areas | Respect religious and sacred places, disease areas, animal taboos |
| Hospitality | Accept what is offered; share equally; eat as they do; finish all food |
| Touching | Be cautious — many cultures consider touching taboo |
| Physical contact | Avoid sexual contact |
| Disease avoidance | Build separate shelter; prepare own food and drink if possible without offense |
| Promises | Keep all promises made |
| Payment | Pay for food and supplies |
| Privacy | Do not enter a house unless invited |

> **Barter:** In primitive societies, hard coin, matches, tobacco, salt, razor blades, empty containers, or cloth may be worth more than money.

## Changes to Political Allegiance

Political attitudes can shift rapidly. Do not assume populations of hostile countries are friendly simply because they do not show open hostility. Unless briefed otherwise, avoid all contact.
