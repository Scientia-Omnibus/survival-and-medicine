# Chapter 7: Medicine

> Foremost among the many problems that can compromise your survival ability are medical problems resulting from unplanned events, such as accidental injuries or extreme climates. The ability to treat yourself increases your morale and aids in your survival. Without qualified medical personnel available, you must know what to do to stay alive.

## Requirements for Maintenance of Health

### Water

Your body loses 2-3 liters of water daily through normal processes. Dehydration results from inadequate replacement.

**Effects of Fluid Loss**

| Fluid Loss | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| 5% | Thirst, irritability, nausea, weakness |
| 10% | Dizziness, headache, inability to walk, tingling in limbs |
| 15% | Dim vision, painful urination, swollen tongue, deafness, numb skin |
| >15% | May result in death |

**Signs of Dehydration:** Dark urine with strong odor, low urine output, dark sunken eyes, fatigue, emotional instability, loss of skin elasticity, delayed capillary refill, trench line down center of tongue, thirst (last to appear - you are already 2% dehydrated).

**Fluid Loss Estimation by Vital Signs**

| Fluid Loss | Wrist Pulse | Breathing Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0.75 L | <100 bpm | 12-20/min |
| 0.75-1.5 L | 100-120 bpm | 20-30/min |
| 1.5-2 L | 120-140 bpm | 30-40/min |

Vital signs above these rates require advanced care.

**Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS):** Mix 0.5 tsp salt + 2 tbsp sugar in 1 L water.

| Age | ORS per 24 hours |
|---|---|
| 0-2 years | 0.5 L |
| 2-9 years | 1 L |
| 9+ years | 3 L |

**Prevention:** Drink water when eating. Acclimatize. Conserve sweat, not water. Ration water until a source is found. Drink small amounts at regular intervals. Maintain urine output of at least 0.5 L per 24h. In extreme climates, drink 8-12 oz every 30 minutes. Do not exceed 1.5 qt per hour (risk of overhydration leading to cerebral and pulmonary edema).

### Food

Without food, mental and physical capabilities deteriorate. The average person needs 2,000 calories per day minimum.

| Source | Description |
|---|---|
| Plants | Provide carbohydrates. Dry by wind, air, sun, or fire to preserve. Nuts and seeds provide protein and oils. |
| Animals | More nourishing than plants. Seek insects, crustaceans, mollusks, fish, reptiles first while setting traps for larger game. |
| Fungus | Additional food source. |

### Personal Hygiene

| Practice | Method |
|---|---|
| Daily cleaning | Cloth and soapy water. Focus on feet, armpits, crotch, hands, hair. If no water, air bath (1 hr sun exposure). |
| Hand washing | After handling contaminants, after bathroom, before food. Keep nails trimmed. |
| Hair care | Keep clean, combed, trimmed to avoid parasites. |
| Clothing | Clean when soiled. Air clothing 2 hours if water scarce. Turn sleeping bag inside out after use. |
| Dental | Brush daily. Chewing stick or cloth if no brush. Temporary fillings: candle wax, tobacco, toothpaste, gingerroot. |
| Foot care | Break in boots. Wash and massage daily. Trim toenails straight. Blisters: do not open if intact; pad around. For large blisters, thread drain method. |
| Rest | 10 min per hour minimum. Alternate mental and physical activity. |
| Campsite | Use latrines or dig cat holes. Collect water upstream from camp. Purify all water. |

## Medical Emergencies

**Breathing Problems** can be caused by: foreign matter in airway, face/neck injuries, inflammation from smoke/allergens, neck bent forward, tongue blocking airway when unconscious.

**Severe Bleeding:** 1 L loss = moderate shock. 2 L = severe shock. 3 L = usually fatal.

**Shock:** Clinical condition when cardiac output is insufficient to supply organs and tissues.

## Lifesaving Steps

Control panic. Perform rapid exam. Follow ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation).

### Open Airway

1. Check for obstruction. If victim can cough/speak, let them clear it naturally.
2. Sweep mouth clear of foreign objects, broken teeth, dentures.
3. Use jaw thrust method: grasp lower jaw angles, lift forward.
4. Pinch nose, give two breaths. Look for chest rise, listen for air, feel for flow.
5. If no spontaneous breathing, perform mouth-to-mouth.
6. Check mouth periodically for vomit.

### Control Bleeding

| Type | Source | Color | Flow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Arterial | Arteries (away from heart) | Bright red | Spurts/pulses with heartbeat |
| Venous | Veins (returning to heart) | Dark red, maroon, bluish | Steady flow |
| Capillary | Small vessels | - | Slow, minor |

**Control Methods (in order of use):**

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| Direct Pressure | Most effective. Apply firm pressure directly over wound. Maintain for 30 min. If bleeding continues, apply pressure dressing. Do not remove, even when blood-soaked. Leave 1-2 days. |
| Elevation | Raise extremity above heart. Use with direct pressure. |
| Pressure Points | Apply digital pressure where artery passes over bone near wound. Use caution on neck. |
| Digital Ligation | Press finger on bleeding end of vein or artery. |
| Tourniquet | Last resort. Place between wound and heart, 5-10 cm above wound. Never over wound or fracture. Lone survivor: do not remove. Buddy: release every 10-15 min for 1-2 min. |

> **WARNING**
> Use caution when applying pressure to the neck. Too much pressure for too long may cause unconsciousness or death. Never place a tourniquet around the neck.

### Prevent and Treat Shock

| Condition | Position |
|---|---|
| Conscious | Level surface, elevate legs 15-20 cm |
| Unconscious | Side or abdomen, head turned to prevent choking |
| Unsure | Lay flat. Do not move once positioned. |

Maintain body heat. Replace wet clothing. Provide shelter. Warm liquids/foods if conscious. No fluids if unconscious or abdominal wounds. Rest for at least 24 hours.

## Bone and Joint Injury

### Fractures

| Type | Description | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Open (compound) | Bone protrudes through skin | Clean with antiseptic, keep moist, splint, monitor blood flow |
| Closed | No open wound | Immobilize, splint |

**Signs:** Pain, tenderness, discoloration, swelling, deformity, loss of function, grating.

**Dangers:** Severing or compression of nerve/blood vessel. Minimum manipulation. If area below break becomes numb, swollen, cool, or pale - major vessel may be severed. Reset fracture, treat for shock, replace fluids.

### Dislocations

Bones out of proper alignment. Treat by reduction (manual traction), immobilization (splint 7-14 days), rehabilitation.

### Sprains

Overstretched tendon or ligament. **RICE** method:

| Letter | Action |
|---|---|
| R | Rest injured area |
| I | Ice for 24-48 hours (cold spring water if ice unavailable) |
| C | Compression wrap or splint |
| E | Elevate affected area |

## Bites and Stings

### Disease-Carrying Pests

| Pest | Diseases |
|---|---|
| Ticks | Rocky Mountain spotted fever, Lyme disease |
| Mosquitoes | Malaria, dengue |
| Flies | Sleeping sickness, typhoid, cholera, dysentery |
| Fleas | Plague |
| Lice | Typhus, relapsing fever |

**Prevention:** Keep immunizations current, avoid infested areas, use netting and repellent, wear proper clothing.

**Tick removal:** Cover with petroleum jelly, heavy oil, or tree sap to cut off air. Remove with tweezers at mouthparts. Do not squeeze body. Clean wound daily.

**Bee/Wasp stings:** Remove stinger by scraping with fingernail or knife. Do not squeeze. Wash thoroughly.

**Itch relief:** Cold compresses, mud/ash paste, dandelion sap, coconut meat, crushed garlic, onion.

### Spider and Scorpion Bites

| Type | Identification | Symptoms | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black Widow | Red hourglass on abdomen | Severe local pain spreading to body, abdominal cramps, nausea, weakness, tremors, sweating | Treat shock, CPR-ready, clean wound, antivenin available |
| Funnel Web | Large brown/gray (Australia) | Same as black widow | Same as black widow |
| Brown Recluse | Dark brown violin on back | Painless bite, red area, deep purple necrosis within 3-4 days, persistent ulcer | Clean wound, treat systemic reactions |
| Tarantula | Large, hairy (tropics) | Pain, bleeding, infection risk | Treat as open wound, prevent infection |
| Scorpion | All poisonous | Local reaction (pain, swelling) or systemic (respiratory, spasms, heart failure) | Treat as black widow bite |

### Snakebites

**If bitten by a poisonous snake:**

1. Reassure victim, keep still
2. Set up for shock, force fluids
3. Remove watches, rings, constricting items
4. Clean bite area
5. Maintain airway, prepare for CPR
6. Apply constricting band between wound and heart
7. Immobilize site
8. Use mechanical suction device for minimum 30 minutes

> **WARNING**
> - Do NOT give alcohol, tobacco, atropine, morphine, or CNS depressors
> - Do NOT make deep cuts at bite site (opens capillaries to bloodstream)
> - Do NOT touch face or rub eyes (venom may cause blindness)
> - Do NOT break large blisters around bite site

**Aftercare:** If infection appears, keep wound open and clean. Use heat after 24-48 hours. Keep wound covered with dry sterile dressing. Victim should drink large amounts of fluids until infection clears.

## Wounds

### Open Wounds

**Cleaning:** Remove/cut clothing from wound. Check for exit wound. Clean surrounding skin. Rinse with water under pressure (fresh urine if water unavailable).

**Treatment:** Use open treatment method - do not suture. Cover with clean dressing, change daily. For gaping wounds, butterfly closure with extreme caution.

**Infected Wound Treatment:**
- Warm, moist compress for 30 min, 3-4 times daily
- Drain and gently probe with sterile instrument
- Dress and bandage
- Drink plenty of water
- Continue daily until infection clears

**Natural Antiseptics:**

| Substance | Use |
|---|---|
| Iodine tablets | 5-15 tablets in 1 L water for wound rinse |
| Garlic | Rub on wound or boil and use water |
| Salt water | 2-3 tbsp per L water to kill bacteria |
| Bee honey | Use straight or dissolved in water |
| Sphagnum moss | Natural iodine source, use as dressing |
| Sugar | Place on wound, remove when glazed, reapply |

### Burns

1. Stop burning process (remove clothing, douse with water/sand, roll on ground)
2. Soak dressings in tannic acid solution (tea, inner bark of hardwood, acorns) for 10 min
3. Cool dressings and apply over burns. Sugar and honey also work.
4. Treat as open wound
5. Replace fluid loss (oral preferred, 1-1.5 L/hr via rectal route if needed)
6. Maintain open airway
7. Treat for shock

For white phosphorous burns: pick out with tweezers. Do not douse with water.

## Environmental Injuries

### Heatstroke

Body temp >40.5°C (105°F). Symptoms: swollen red face, reddened whites of eyes, no sweating, unconsciousness or delirium. Cool rapidly by immersion or cool compresses to neck, armpits, groin, and head.

Expect during cooling: vomiting, diarrhea, struggling, shivering, prolonged unconsciousness, rebound heatstroke within 48 hours, cardiac arrest. Be ready for CPR. Treat for dehydration with lightly salted water.

### Cold Injuries

| Injury | Cause | Symptoms | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chilblains/Frostnip | Freezing temperatures, wind chill | Firm, cold, white/gray areas on face, ears, extremities; may blister | Warm with hands or warm object |
| Trench Foot | Wet/damp conditions just above freezing | Nerve and muscle damage, possible gangrene | Keep feet dry, carry extra socks, wash and dry feet daily |
| Frostbite | Frozen tissues below skin | Solid, immovable tissue | Immerse in 37-42°C water until thawed. Do not use open flame. |
| Hypothermia | Core temp <36°C (97°F) | Body failure to maintain temperature | Shelter, remove wet clothes, warm fluids, skin-to-skin contact |

### Diarrhea

Treatments:
- Limit fluid intake for 24 hours
- Strong tea solution (tannic acid) every 2 hours
- Chalk/charcoal/bone solution: 2 tbsp every 2 hours

### Intestinal Parasites

| Remedy | Dosage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salt water | 4 tbsp salt in 1 L water | Do not repeat |
| Tobacco | 1-1.5 cigarettes or 1 tsp smokeless | Repeat in 24-48h if severe |
| Kerosene | 2 tbsp | Repeat in 24-48h if needed. Do not inhale fumes. |
| Hot peppers | As part of steady diet | Prevents parasitic attachment |
| Garlic | 4 crushed cloves in 1 glass liquid daily | For 3 weeks |

> **WARNING**
> Tobacco and kerosene treatments are very dangerous. Use with extreme caution.

## Herbal Medicines

> **WARNING**
> Use herbal medicines with extreme care, and only when you lack or have limited medical supplies. Some herbal medicines are dangerous and may cause further damage or even death.
