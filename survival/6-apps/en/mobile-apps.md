### Morse Signal

Morse code can be transmitted without special gear using reflected light (mirror or tilted phone screen), sound of strikes on metal, or a horn. It can broadcast farther than voice with less energy.

The SOS distress signal is: dot dot dot, dash dash dash, dot dot dot (... --- ...)

### FM Radio

Many phones come with an FM radio receiver. The headphone cable typically serves as the antenna, but any 3.5 mm jack component (microphone, loudspeaker, extension cord) can work. Ensure the radio app redirects sound to the phone's integrated speaker if using a non-sound-producing plug.

### Readers / Offline Library

Phones can hold thousands of textbooks. Download PDFs and eBooks in advance. Many public domain works are available for download.

### Maps / Navigation

Have a mapping solution that works offline. Download data for your area and test it in airplane mode. GPS may take longer to acquire a signal without network assistance.

### Camera

Use your camera to capture information you may not remember accurately under stress. Take pictures of locations to help find your way back.

### Recorder

Keeping a diary helps combat loneliness and maintain willpower. A phone can serve as a voice recorder if paper is unavailable.
