> Insects are often overlooked as a danger. More people die annually from bee stings and anaphylactic shock than from snake bites. The greatest danger is often disease transmission.

## Dangerous Arthropods

| Species | Description | Habitat | Distribution | Danger / Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scorpion | Dull brown, yellow, or black. 7.5-20 cm. Lobsterlike pincers, jointed tail held over back. | Decaying matter, under debris, logs, rocks. Feeds at night. Hides in boots. | Worldwide: temperate, arid, tropical | Sting causes local pain, swelling, possible incapacitation, death |
| Brown recluse spider | Brown to black with fiddle mark on back. Chunky body, slim legs 2.5-4 cm. | Under debris, rocks, logs. Caves, dark places. | North America | Bite causes local tissue damage |
| Funnelweb spider | Large, brown, bulky. Aggressive when disturbed. | Woods, jungles, brushy areas. Funnel-shaped web. | Australia | Dangerous bite; seek medical aid |
| Tarantula | Very large, brown, black, reddish, hairy. Large fangs. | Desert areas, tropics. | Americas, southern Europe | Painful bite; low toxicity to humans |
| Widow spider | Dark with red or orange markings on female abdomen. | Under logs, rocks, debris. Shaded places. | Varied species worldwide | Female venomous. Red widow (Middle East) deadly to man |
| Centipede | Multi-joined body up to 30 cm. Dull orange to brown. | Under bark and stones. Active at night. | Worldwide | Bite painful; low fatality risk |
| Bee | Brown or black hairy bodies. Live in colonies, build wax combs. | Hollow trees, caves, dwellings. Near water in desert. | Worldwide | Barbed stinger — dies after stinging. Risk of anaphylactic shock |
| Wasps and hornets | Smooth-bodied, slender. Mud or paper nests. Smooth stinger allows multiple attacks. | Anywhere. | Worldwide | Multiple stings possible. Risk of allergic reaction |
| Tick | Round body, pinhead to 2.5 cm. 8 legs, sucking mouth parts. | Forests, grasslands, urban areas, farmlands. | Worldwide | Disease transmission (Lyme, etc.). Remove with tweezers |

> **First aid for stings and bites:** Remove stinger if present. Clean wound with soap and water. Apply cold pack to reduce swelling. Monitor for signs of allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling of face or throat). Seek medical help if severe reaction occurs.
